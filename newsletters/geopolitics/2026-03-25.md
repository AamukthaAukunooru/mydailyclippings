The global geopolitical landscape continues to exhibit significant dynamism, marked by persistent conflicts, evolving diplomatic overtures, and economic adjustments. Escalating tensions in the **Middle East** remain a primary concern, driving commodity price volatility and prompting international mediation efforts. Concurrently, shifts in diplomatic strategies are observable in various regions, as nations navigate complex relationships and pursue national interests amidst a fragmented international order. The economic repercussions of these developments, particularly regarding energy and critical resources, are a central theme for policymakers and markets worldwide.

## Middle East Tensions and Global Economic Strain

The conflict involving the **United States**, **Israel**, and **Iran** continues to be a focal point, with ongoing military operations and significant regional and global implications. The tempo of combat operations is expected to persist for at least another two to four weeks, involving strikes on **Iranian** military leadership, security, and nuclear assets by the **US** and **Israel**. In response, **Iran** has reportedly used uncrewed aerial vehicles (UAVs) and missile strikes targeting **US** military assets and critical infrastructure in **Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC)** countries and **Israel**. **Iran's** new Supreme Leader, **Mojtaba Khamenei**, has stated that **Iran** will maintain strikes on **US** bases and enforce a blockade of the **Strait of Hormuz**. **Iranian** military actions have disrupted shipping through the **Strait of Hormuz**, a critical chokepoint for global oil supply, with maritime tracking data indicating a 96% decrease in transits by most vessel types between March 1 and March 14, compared to the preceding ten days. Some vessels affiliated with **China**, **Bangladesh**, **Turkey**, and **India** have reportedly been permitted passage.

This escalation has had a pronounced effect on global energy markets, with oil prices surging and Brent crude reportedly spiking to $119 per barrel. Governments are facing pressure to secure alternative energy supplies, leading to measures such as fuel export bans, price caps, and the release of 400 million barrels from emergency reserves by the **International Energy Agency**. The **US** has also issued sanctions waivers for the purchase of **Russian** and **Iranian** oil currently at sea, with **US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent** noting that these measures aim to increase the physical supply of oil to offset disruptions in the **Strait of Hormuz**. Financial leaders are advocating for increased resilience in the face of these geopolitical shifts, even if it entails accepting greater risk. Concerns about the fragility of the global food system due to potential disruptions at chokepoints like the **Strait of Hormuz** have also been highlighted.

Amidst these tensions, **Egypt** has initiated a diplomatic offensive to prevent the **Middle East** from descending into "total chaos," advocating for dialogue between the **United States** and **Iran** as the sole path to regional stability. **Egyptian Foreign Minister Badr Abdelatty** held high-level consultations with counterparts from **Jordan**, **Saudi Arabia**, the **United Arab Emirates**, **Oman**, **Turkey**, **Pakistan**, **France**, and **Cyprus**, as well as **US Special Envoy for the Middle East Steve Witkoff**. These discussions focused on de-escalation, with **Cairo** emphasizing that a negotiating path between **Tehran** and **Washington** is crucial. Separately, **Israel** has approved a proposal to allocate land in **Jerusalem** for the construction of a permanent **United States Embassy**, a move described by **Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu** and other officials as a "major diplomatic step".

## Shifting Diplomatic Landscapes and Resource Security

Beyond the **Middle East**, several other diplomatic and resource-related developments are noteworthy. In a significant shift in **US** foreign policy, **Belarus** dictator **Alyaksandr Lukashenka** released 250 political prisoners on March 19 in exchange for **US** sanctions relief. This marks the latest in a series of agreements brokered by **US President Donald Trump's Special Envoy for Belarus John Coale**, aimed at reviving diplomatic ties with **Minsk**. The lifting of **US** sanctions on three major **Belarusian** fertilizer industry companies comes as the **Iran** conflict drives up fertilizer prices globally. While this initiative has brought humanitarian benefits, concerns persist among human rights groups that it could incentivize **Belarusian** authorities to imprison more domestic opponents.

In **US-China** relations, there are indications that **Washington** and **Beijing** prefer transactional diplomacy over open rupture. **US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent** is scheduled to meet with **Chinese Vice Premier He Lifeng** in **France** ahead of a potential summit between **President Trump** and **President Xi**. **Beijing's** foreign minister has expressed hope that 2026 could be a "landmark year" for relations with the **United States**, suggesting a focus on tone management rather than strategic convergence.

Resource security is also gaining prominence. In **Venezuela**, **US** economic interest has extended to mining, leading to reduced regulation in the country's revised mining law, including renewed access to international arbitration. However, international mining companies are likely to face considerable security risks due to local control by non-state armed groups. The **Arctic** region is increasingly being viewed as a live operational theater, as demonstrated by the **Arctic Edge 2026** exercises across **Alaska** and **Greenland**, focusing on cruise-missile defense, counter-drone operations, and infrastructure protection. **Canada** has also announced C$32 billion in new spending for defense and infrastructure in its North, signaling that northern sovereignty is becoming a national security priority. Separately, global supply concerns over tungsten, a critical material for semiconductor manufacturing, are intensifying as **Middle East** geopolitical tensions disrupt commodity markets and drive up prices. The **World Gold Council (WGC)** reports that additional central banks are expected to increase gold purchases this year as a hedge against geopolitical risks and de-dollarization.
