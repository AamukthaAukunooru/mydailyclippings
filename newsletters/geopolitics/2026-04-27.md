## Middle East Tensions and Global Maritime Security

The conflict involving the **United States**, **Israel**, and **Iran** continues to significantly impact global shipping, particularly through the **Strait of Hormuz**. This vital waterway has experienced blockades and slowed traffic, leading to a substantial global supply chain crisis for essential goods such as oil, fertilizers, and pharmaceuticals. **TotalEnergies** has warned that a prolonged conflict could trigger global energy shortages.

In response to these disruptions, **Germany** and **France** are actively advocating for the reopening of the **Strait of Hormuz**. **German Defense Minister Boris Pistorius** has indicated his country's intention to deploy naval ships to the Mediterranean to be positioned for a potential international mission to secure shipping in the strait. **French President Emmanuel Macron**, speaking in Athens, reaffirmed his efforts to achieve a full reopening of the waterway in accordance with international law, emphasizing freedom of navigation.

Diplomatic efforts to de-escalate the conflict are ongoing, though direct talks between the **United States** and **Iran** have reportedly stalled. **Pakistan** has emerged as a key mediator, hosting **Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Iraqi** for discussions with **Pakistani** civilian and military leadership aimed at ceasefire stability and exploring the path forward for talks with the **US**. However, **Iran** has expressed skepticism about **Washington's** intentions, with **Iraqi** openly questioning the **United States'** seriousness regarding diplomacy. **Iran** has also accused the **United States** of seeking a "face-saving exit" from the war. The **UN's International Maritime Organization (IMO)** Secretary-General **Arsenio Dominguez** highlighted how ships and seafarers have become "leverage in geopolitical disputes," with up to 20,000 seafarers reportedly stranded in the **Persian Gulf** since the conflict began in late February. The military escalation in the **Middle East** is also estimated by the **United Nations Development Programme (UNDP)** to cost economies in the region between 3.7% and 6.0% of their collective Gross Domestic Product (GDP). **Malaysian Prime Minister Datuk Seri Anwar Ibrahim** noted that the "Israeli-American aggression against Iran" is causing significant economic problems globally, demanding a recalibration of economic priorities amid heightened uncertainty.

## Asia-Pacific Dynamics: China's Influence and Taiwan's Sovereignty

In Southeast Asia, **China** continues to strengthen its regional ties. **Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi** visited **Myanmar's** capital, Naypyitaw, meeting with **Min Aung Hlaing**, the leader of **Myanmar's** military-backed government. The discussions focused on enhancing **Myanmar's** international relations and cooperation within the **Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN)**. This visit follows **Min Aung Hlaing's** swearing-in as president after an election that critics describe as neither free nor fair, designed to maintain the military's grip on power. **China**, a major trading partner and arms supplier to **Myanmar**, has openly supported the recent elections.

Meanwhile, **Taiwan's** diplomatic space is facing increasing pressure. **Taiwanese President William Lai Ching-te's** planned visit to diplomatic ally **Eswatini** was canceled after **Mauritius**, **Madagascar**, and **Seychelles** revoked overflight permissions for his aircraft. This action is seen as an unprecedented tactic by the **People's Republic of China (PRC)** to disrupt **Taiwan's** diplomatic engagements and erode its sovereignty. The ministries of foreign affairs of **Seychelles** and **Madagascar** stated that the revocations were in line with their "one China policy". **President Lai** asserted that the **PRC** pressured these countries, with a senior **Taiwanese** security official suggesting threats to revoke debt relief were involved. In a rare display of partisan solidarity, **Taiwan's** opposition **Kuomintang (KMT)** and **Taiwan People's Party (TPP)** joined the ruling **Democratic Progressive Party (DPP)** in condemning the **PRC's** coercion.

## Global Military Spending and the Future of AI Governance

According to new data released by the **Stockholm International Peace Research Institute (SIPRI)** on April 27, 2026, world military expenditure reached **$2887 billion** in 2025, marking an increase of 2.9% in real terms over 2024. While military spending in the **United States** declined by 7.5% due to no new financial military assistance for **Ukraine** during the year, expenditures in **Europe** surged by 14% and in **Asia and Oceania** by 8.1%. The top three military spenders globally in 2025 were the **USA**, **China**, and **Russia**, collectively accounting for 51% of the total. This marks the eleventh consecutive year of rising global military expenditure, driven by ongoing wars, uncertainty, and geopolitical upheaval.

Another crucial decision concerns export controls on advanced **AI** chips and semiconductor manufacturing equipment, which will determine who can train frontier models at scale and influence whether **AI** becomes a domain of managed competition or technological decoupling.
