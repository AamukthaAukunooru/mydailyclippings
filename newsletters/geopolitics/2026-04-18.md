## Middle East Tensions: Iran War, Ceasefire, and the Strait of Hormuz

The "war in **Iran**" has remained a central point of discussion, with analyses exploring its geopolitical ramifications, especially concerning the **US-China** rivalry. **US President Donald Trump** stated that a deal to conclude the conflict, which the **US** and **Israel** initiated with **Iran** in late February, is "mostly complete," with further negotiations for a lasting agreement anticipated this weekend. **President Trump** also indicated that **Iran** has agreed to indefinitely suspend its nuclear program and will not receive frozen funds from the **US**. However, some reports suggest that recent talks in **Islamabad** between **Washington** and **Tehran** have failed, implying a potential resurgence of the **Iran** war.

A 10-day ceasefire between **Israel** and **Lebanon** commenced on Friday, a pause that could contribute to broader peace talks involving **Iran** and the **United States**. **President Trump** announced this ceasefire following discussions with leaders from both countries, expressing an intention to invite them to the **White House** for "meaningful talks." **Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu** confirmed the agreement, and **Prime Minister Nawaf Salam** of **Lebanon** welcomed the development.

The status of the **Strait of Hormuz**, a vital maritime chokepoint, continues to be a point of contention. **Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi** declared the strait fully open to commercial vessels, a statement that reportedly led to a decline in US crude oil (WTI) prices. Conversely, **President Trump** affirmed the continuation of a **US** blockade on **Iranian** ships and ports until **Tehran** reaches a comprehensive deal with the **US**, including on its nuclear program. The **US** military is enforcing a naval blockade, and the **Pentagon** is deploying additional troops to the **Middle East**. Perspectives on the **Iran** conflict's outcomes vary, with some suggesting that despite being battered, the **Iranian** regime has endured and is asserting new claims over the **Strait of Hormuz**. Paradoxically, **Iran** reportedly gained sanctions relief during the conflict that was previously unattainable. The **UK Prime Minister** met **French President Emmanuel Macron** in **Paris** to discuss the Middle East situation and the **Strait of Hormuz Maritime Freedom of Navigation Initiative**.

## Global Economic Shifts: From Efficiency to Resilience

A notable trend in the global economy is the shift by countries from efficiency-driven models towards resilience in their trade and investment strategies, a move largely prompted by rising geopolitical uncertainty. **Malaysia's Finance Minister II Datuk Seri Amir Hamzah Azizan** underscored this paradigm shift during the 2026 Spring Meetings of the **International Monetary Fund** and the **World Bank Group**. He noted that while geopolitical stability previously underpinned global economic expansion, allowing for optimized operations, the increasing unpredictability of global conditions necessitates a "just-in-case" approach over the traditional "just-in-time" model.

Governments are now prioritizing the strengthening of core capabilities and domestic capacity to enhance resilience against external shocks. The **COVID-19** pandemic notably exposed vulnerabilities in global supply chains, particularly for critical goods. In response, countries are expanding external engagements and forging new partnerships to mitigate overdependence on single sources. The **US-Israeli** strikes on **Iran** have been identified as the seventh major fossil fuel price shock since the 1970s, highlighting the systemic threat that reliance on fossil fuels poses to financial stability. This has led to the perspective that the transition to renewable energy is not merely a climate initiative but a crucial monetary policy imperative, offering energy sovereignty and shielding economies from volatile "fossilflation" cycles.

## Asia-Pacific Efforts Towards Nuclear Disarmament

In the **Asia-Pacific** region, government officials representing over 20 nuclear-weapon-free nations convened in **Jakarta, Indonesia**, this week to address the pressing need for the elimination of nuclear weapons. The one-day conference, held on April 17, was co-hosted by **Indonesia** and funded by **Austria**, in collaboration with the **International Campaign to Abolish Nuclear Weapons (ICAN)** and the **International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC)**.

Discussions at the conference focused on regional and global efforts to implement the **UN Treaty on the Prohibition of Nuclear Weapons (TPNW)**, which entered into force in 2021. Co-organizers issued a warning that in the current geopolitical climate, marked by increased rivalries and insecurities, the risk of nuclear weapons use is growing, with potentially catastrophic and far-reaching consequences. **Ambassador George-Wilhelm Gallhofer** of **Austria** emphasized that the "only effective measure to eliminate the risk stemming from nuclear weapons is abolition" and praised the **TPNW** for reinforcing the "legal and normative taboo against the possession and use of nuclear weapons".
