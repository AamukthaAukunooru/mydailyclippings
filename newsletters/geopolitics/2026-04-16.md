International reactions to these moves underscore a complex web of alliances and differing approaches to regional stability and global trade.

## Middle East Tensions and Diplomatic Overtures

The **United States** has initiated a naval blockade of all **Iranian** ports, a move **US President Donald Trump** described as an effort to pressure **Iran** into reopening the crucial **Strait of Hormuz** and resuming negotiations to end the ongoing conflict. **President Trump** has accused **Iran** of "economic terrorism" and stated that "two can play at that game". The blockade, which began on Monday, April 14, 2026, has seen the deployment of over 15 **US** warships, including an aircraft carrier, to enforce restrictions on access to **Iranian** ports. **US Central Command** reported that during the first 24 hours of the blockade, no ships made it through, and six were turned back. **Iran** has condemned the blockade as a violation of its sovereignty and has threatened to target all ports in the **Persian Gulf** and **Gulf of Oman** in retaliation.

Despite the escalation, there are indications that both the **US** and **Iran** are considering resuming face-to-face negotiations before a two-week ceasefire is set to expire early next week. **President Trump** expressed optimism, noting that **Iran** had reached out to his administration. However, several complex issues remain on the table beyond the nuclear program, including **Iran's** ballistic missile program and its support for proxies in the region, which are considered major concerns for **Israel** and regional countries.

The economic ramifications of the conflict and the blockade are significant. Oil prices have been nearing the $100 a barrel mark, leading to warnings from the **International Monetary Fund (IMF)** that the global economy is at risk of its slowest growth since the COVID-19 pandemic if crude oil prices remain high. The **IMF** forecasts a potential drop in global growth to 2.5% and an increase in inflation to around 5.5% under an adverse scenario of sustained high oil prices. The **European Bank for Reconstruction and Development** has also cautioned about a more serious economic impact on the **EU** if the conflict escalates further.

## International Responses and European Divergence

The **US** blockade has drawn sharp international reactions. **China's** foreign ministry has condemned the blockade of **Iranian** ports as "dangerous and irresponsible," warning that it will only "exacerbate tensions". **China** has also called on the international community to step up efforts to promote peace talks. Separately, **Chinese President Xi Jinping** met with **Sheikh Khaled bin Mohamed bin Zayed Al Nahyan**, Crown Prince of **Abu Dhabi, UAE**, on April 14, discussing mutual support, economic cooperation, and the situation in the **Middle East**, with **President Xi** stressing **China's** principled position of promoting peace and facilitating talks.

In **Europe**, there is a clear divergence from the **US** approach to the **Strait of Hormuz**. **Britain** and **France** have announced they will co-host a meeting on maritime security in the strait this week, reflecting growing European concern over the waterway's closure and its economic fallout. However, **British Prime Minister Keir Starmer** explicitly stated that **Britain** does not support **President Trump's** proposed blockade, emphasizing that **Britain** would not be "dragged into the war" which is not in its national interest. **French President Emmanuel Macron** echoed this sentiment, stressing that a proposed multinational mission to restore free navigation would be strictly defensive and separate from parties to the conflict. **Italy** has also aligned with this broader European position, with **Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni** warning of "unpredictable economic consequences" if **Iran** were to impose additional transit duties. The **EU's** new entry-exit system also caused travel chaos at airport border controls over the weekend, leading to long queues for passengers.

## Humanitarian Crisis in Sudan

Away from the immediate tensions in the **Middle East**, the humanitarian situation in **Sudan** continues to deteriorate. The war in the country has now entered its fourth year, making it the world's largest humanitarian and displacement crisis. Foreign ministers from across the world convened in **Berlin** on Wednesday, April 15, to demonstrate support for **Sudan** as humanitarian needs deepen. **UN** relief chiefs have warned that the world is failing **Sudan**, calling for an end to the conflict between rival militaries.
