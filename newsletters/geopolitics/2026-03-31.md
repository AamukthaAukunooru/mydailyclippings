The geopolitical landscape remains highly dynamic, with a significant escalation of tensions in the Middle East dominating global attention. The ongoing conflict involving the **United States**, **Israel**, and **Iran** has led to substantial disruptions in global energy markets and raised alarms regarding regional stability and nuclear proliferation. Concurrently, the protracted conflict in **Eastern Europe** continues, with new offensives and impacts on critical global resources.

## Escalation in the Middle East and Global Energy Fallout

The conflict involving the **United States**, **Israel**, and **Iran** has intensified, leading to widespread geopolitical and economic repercussions. **Yemeni Houthi** forces have reportedly expanded their involvement, launching attacks against **Israel**, further widening the regional scope of the conflict. **Iran** has conducted strikes on energy infrastructure in **Saudi Arabia**, **Kuwait**, and **Qatar**, contributing to a volatile energy market.

A critical flashpoint remains the **Strait of Hormuz**, a vital maritime chokepoint. The strait has experienced significant restrictions, with maritime activity described as entering a "more restrictive and unpredictable phase". This disruption has severely inflated global oil prices, with **Brent** crude reportedly heading for a record monthly increase of approximately 58%. **US President Trump** has indicated a willingness to consider destroying **Iranian** oil infrastructure if the **Strait of Hormuz** remains closed. Reports also suggest that **Israel** has proposed to **President Trump** that striking **Iran's** energy infrastructure could accelerate regime change by triggering a financial collapse.

**Egyptian President Abdel-Fattah El-Sisi** has urged **President Trump** to de-escalate the conflict, warning of severe global energy and economic fallout. **El-Sisi** described the current situation as "the largest energy crisis in history," surpassing the impact of the **Russia-Ukraine** conflict or the **COVID-19** pandemic on international oil companies. Beyond oil, the conflict is also contributing to rising fertilizer prices, which are expected to significantly affect economies worldwide. The economic anxiety is palpable, with **US Treasury** yields declining as investors await critical employment and manufacturing data to assess the broader economic impact. A recent report by **New York Life Investment Management** underscores this shift, stating that geopolitics is no longer a periodic source of volatility but a structural force driving economic outcomes and market behavior.

## Nuclear Proliferation Concerns and Shifting Global Dynamics

The escalating **Iran war** is placing considerable strain on the global non-proliferation regime. The expiration of the **New START** treaty in February without a replacement, coupled with **China's** expanding nuclear arsenal and **France's** announced expansion of its nuclear program, creates a complex backdrop. There is a growing sentiment in non-nuclear states like **Turkey**, **Poland**, and **South Korea** towards developing domestic nuclear capabilities, driven by perceived vulnerabilities and doubts over **Washington's** ability to uphold its extended deterrence and security commitments to allies. The reported redeployment of **US THAAD** missile defense systems from **South Korea** to the Middle East has further fueled these concerns in **East Asia**.

Observers note a potentially dangerous lesson emerging from the conflict: that nuclear weapons may deter attacks in a way that conventional capabilities cannot. This perspective is gaining traction, particularly given that **Iran** has reportedly been attacked even during active negotiation rounds.

Amidst these tensions, **China** has adopted a strategy described as "strategic patience." While condemning the **US**'s "vicious cycle of chaos" in **Iran**, **Beijing's** approach to the crisis appears focused on stability, economic expansion, and longevity, rather than direct military involvement. **China**, a major energy consumer, relies on stable energy flows from the **Gulf** and is exploring ways to diversify currency usage in international oil trades, including discussions about **Chinese yuan**-denominated oil transactions.

In a separate but notable development reflecting shifting geopolitical alignments, **US President Donald Trump** indicated he has "no problem" with a **Russian** oil tanker delivering relief to **Cuba**, despite the vessel, **Anatoly Kolodkin**, being under sanctions. **Cuba** has been grappling with severe blackouts and a broader crisis due to a **US** oil blockade.

## The Enduring Conflict in Eastern Europe

The conflict in **Eastern Europe** continues to be a significant area of geopolitical concern. **Russia** has launched a new and aggressive spring offensive against **Ukraine**, intensifying the fighting. This renewed push by **Moscow** comes as the Middle East conflict diverts some international attention and resources.

Beyond the direct military impact, the conflict has exacerbated a global fertilizer crisis. **Russia** has reportedly halted its ammonium nitrate exports, a move that is expected to worsen the already precarious global fertilizer supply chain. This development has direct implications for global food security and agricultural production. Adding to regional complexities, **Hungary** has announced it will halt gas deliveries to **Ukraine**. Meanwhile, political shifts within **Europe** are also being observed, with the **Alternative for Germany (AfD)** making gains in **German** state elections, and the **European Union** accusing **Hungary** of "pro-Russian espionage".
