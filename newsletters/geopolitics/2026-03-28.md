## Middle East: Conflict Intensifies Amidst Diplomatic Overtures

The protracted conflict involving the **United States**, **Israel**, and **Iran** continues to dominate headlines, with both military actions and diplomatic initiatives unfolding in the past 48 hours. **US President Donald Trump** extended a pause on striking **Iranian** energy infrastructure until April 6, indicating that talks with **Iran** are "going very well". The **U.S.** administration reportedly presented **Iran** with a 15-point peace proposal, though **Iran** has reportedly rejected this plan and issued a counterproposal, with a formal response expected. Despite these diplomatic signals, both sides appear to be bolstering their forces.

Military operations have continued, with **Israel's** military reporting "wide-scale wave of strikes targeting infrastructure of the **Iranian** terror regime in the heart of **Tehran**" early Friday. **Iran**, in turn, stated its Revolutionary Guards carried out missile and drone strikes targeting sites in **Israel** and military facilities in the **Gulf** used by **U.S.** forces. Reports also indicate that **Iran's** nuclear facilities were targeted, including a heavy-water plant and a yellowcake production plant. The **Israeli Defense Forces (IDF)** announced on March 26 that they killed **Islamic Revolutionary Guards Corps (IRGC) Navy Commander Rear Admiral Alireza Tangsiri** in **Bandar Abbas**, **Hormozgan Province**, who was deemed "directly responsible" for mining and blocking the **Strait of Hormuz**. The **UN** human rights chief has warned that the escalating conflict risks spiraling into a broader crisis, and the prolonged closure of the **Strait of Hormuz** is impacting the movement of oil, gas, and fertilizer. **Kazakhstan** has declared its readiness to provide a venue for peace talks to end the war, with its Foreign Minister **Yermek Kosherbayev** discussing the situation with **Iranian** counterpart **Abbas Araghchi**.

## Russia-Ukraine War: Drone Attacks and International Justice

The conflict between **Russia** and **Ukraine** remains active, with recent developments including **Ukrainian** drone attacks on **Russian** territory and international legal initiatives. For the third consecutive night through March 27, explosions reportedly rocked **Russian** oil terminals in **Leningrad Oblast**, with **Ukrainian** drones striking facilities in the port cities of **Ust-Luga** and **Primorsk**. These attacks follow earlier strikes on an energy terminal in **Ust-Luga** and the **Kirishi Petroleum Organic Synthesis (Kinef) oil refinery**. In response, **Russia's** Defense Ministry claimed to have downed 85 **Ukrainian** drones overnight, including over **Leningrad Oblast** and **Vologda Oblast**.

On the battlefield, recent analysis from March 25, 2026, indicates that **Russian** forces lost 4 square miles of **Ukraine's** territory between February 24 and March 24, 2026, contrasting with gains in the previous four-week period. However, **Russian** troops are also pushing forward in other areas, with an uptick in combat activity reported in the direction of **Huliaypole** and attempts to advance in **Kharkiv** and **Sumy** regions. The **Ukrainian** General Staff reported that **Russia** suffered approximately 1,000 casualties in the past 24 hours as of March 27.

In a significant international development, the **European Commission** adopted a proposal on March 26 initiating the process for the **European Union** to become a founding member of the Special Tribunal for the Crime of Aggression against **Ukraine**. This tribunal aims to prosecute senior political and military leaders for the crime of aggression. The ongoing conflict in the **Middle East** has been noted to deflect global attention from **Russia's** full-scale invasion of **Ukraine**.

## US-China Relations: Anticipated Summit and Lingering Tensions

Relations between the **United States** and **China** are marked by a complex interplay of competition and a desire for stability, with a high-level summit on the horizon. **US President Donald Trump** is expected to visit **Beijing** in mid-May for a summit with **President Xi Jinping**, a meeting that was originally scheduled for late March but was postponed due to the ongoing war in the **Middle East**. **China's** Foreign Minister **Wang Yi** expressed hope earlier this month that 2026 would be a "landmark year" for the relationship between the two countries, despite acknowledged differences.

The upcoming summit is expected to extend beyond trade and economic relations to address strategic and security challenges. While a temporary truce in the trade war has been in place since October, a lasting détente remains uncertain. Issues such as technology controls, sanctions against **Chinese** firms, and restrictions on **Chinese** direct investment in the **United States** are likely to be on **Beijing's** agenda. The delay in the summit underscores the extent to which the **Middle East** conflict is influencing broader geopolitical dynamics, with **Beijing** closely observing its impact on **US-China** relations and **US** domestic politics.
