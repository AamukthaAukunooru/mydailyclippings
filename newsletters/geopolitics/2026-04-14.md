My Daily Clippings

Recent days have seen a continuation of significant geopolitical tensions across multiple regions. The **Middle East** remains a focal point of conflict, with ongoing hostilities and stalled diplomatic efforts raising concerns about global economic stability. In **Eastern Europe**, the conflict in **Ukraine** persists despite attempts at a ceasefire, highlighting the enduring challenges to regional security. Meanwhile, the **Asia-Pacific** region is grappling with internal conflicts and the broader economic ripple effects of global instability.

## The Volatile Middle East and Global Economic Repercussions

The **Middle East** is experiencing a sharp escalation in hostilities, triggered by joint **Israeli**–**US** strikes on **Iran** that began in late February, followed by **Iran's** retaliatory actions across the region. **Israel** and the **United States** have targeted **Iran's** nuclear infrastructure, ballistic missile facilities, and naval assets, reportedly resulting in the deaths of several top military and political officials, including **Iranian Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei** and Secretary of the Supreme National Security Council **Ali Larijani**. In response, **Iran** has launched strikes against **Israel** and **US** bases, as well as allied assets in the **Middle East**. These retaliatory strikes have targeted energy infrastructure in the **Gulf** region, with **Kuwait**, **Bahrain**, **Qatar**, and the **UAE** reporting attacks even after a declared ceasefire.

A significant development is the **US** blockade targeting **Iranian** ports, which went into effect on April 12, adding to fears of further escalation and global trade disruption. This follows **Iran's** declaration on March 4 that it had closed the **Strait of Hormuz**, a critical waterway that typically carries a substantial portion of global maritime petroleum trade. The closure has led to a surge in oil prices, with **Brent** crude surpassing $100 per barrel in mid-March. The **Financial Stability Board (FSB)** has warned that the ongoing conflict and its implications pose significant challenges to global financial stability. The **FSB Chair, Andrew Bailey**, highlighted that vulnerabilities such as stretched asset valuations and liquidity mismatches could interact with heightened financial market volatility, creating a potential "double or triple whammy" threat to stability.

Diplomatic efforts have faced setbacks. Weekend talks between the **United States** and **Iran** in **Islamabad** concluded without an agreement, with **US Vice President JD Vance** stating that **Iran** did not commit to not seeking a nuclear weapon. Control of the **Strait of Hormuz** and **Iran's** uranium stockpiles were key sticking points. Despite a fragile two-week ceasefire agreement between the **US** and **Iran**, hostilities have continued, particularly **Israeli** attacks in **Lebanon**. **Lebanese** authorities reported that deaths from **Israeli** airstrikes have surpassed 2,000 since the escalation began last month.

The economic fallout is far-reaching, with the **Asian Development Bank (ADB)** projecting that the **Middle East** conflict will weigh on the outlook for developing **Asia** and the **Pacific**, leading to moderated growth and higher energy and consumer prices. Disruptions in the **Strait of Hormuz** have impacted global oil supply, with cascading effects on petrochemicals, plastics, fertilizers, and consumer goods.

## Continued Hostilities in Ukraine Amidst Ceasefire Attempts

The conflict in **Ukraine** has seen continued fighting despite a unilateral short-term ceasefire declared by the **Kremlin** for the **Orthodox Easter** holiday on April 11 and 12. Both **Ukrainian** and **Russian** sources accused each other of limited violations during this period. **Ukraine's** General Staff reported that **Russia** violated the ceasefire almost 11,000 times during a 32-hour period. **Russian** authorities, conversely, accused **Ukraine** of breaching the ceasefire with drone strikes in **Russia's Kursk** and **Belgorod oblasts**.

On the ground, **Ukrainian** forces have reportedly pulled back near **Myropilske** in **Sumy Oblast** due to intensive military operations and the opponent's advantage. This area, bordering **Russia's Kursk** region, has seen a buildup of **Russian** troops, and the "gray zone" has expanded. **Russian** forces are also reported to have forcibly deported civilians from newly seized villages in northern pockets of **Sumy Oblast**.

**Ukrainian** forces continue to repel **Russian** attacks across the front line, with heavy fighting ongoing around **Pokrovsk** in **Donetsk Oblast**, where **Ukrainian** forces repelled 28 **Russian** assaults. **Russia** has continued offensive operations in various directions, including **Kharkiv**, **Hulyaipole**, and **Slovyansk**, though without significant advances in all areas. **Ukraine** has intensified its drone and sea-drone campaign against **Russian** oil infrastructure, reportedly damaging or destroying about 20% of **Russia's** refining capacity since early 2024.

In a related development, **Hungary's** recent parliamentary election on April 12 saw a change in leadership, with **Peter Magyar's** party winning and **Prime Minister Viktor Orbán** conceding defeat. **Magyar** described **Moscow** as a "security risk" and, while not supporting **Ukraine's** fast-track entry to the **EU**, signaled acceptance of the €90 billion **EU** loan deal to **Kyiv**, albeit with a request for **Hungary** to be exempt from contributing.

## Shifting Dynamics in the Asia-Pacific and Global Economic Outlook

The **Asia-Pacific** region continues to face various security and economic challenges. Fighting between **Afghanistan** and **Pakistan** resumed after a brief ceasefire, with clashes primarily along the disputed **Durand Line**. Preliminary talks between the two countries in **China** aimed at a comprehensive agreement are ongoing. In **Pakistan's Balochistan** province, separatist violence reached its highest level in recent years, marked by an increase in smaller-scale attacks on security posts and ambushes on army convoys. Militants also maintained a blockade on a section of the **China-Pakistan Economic Corridor** route in March.

**Myanmar** is experiencing increasingly deadly airstrikes by the military, with significant civilian casualties reported in various townships. The coup leader is reportedly set to assume the presidency amidst this escalating violence.

Economically, **Asia-Pacific** economies are facing a challenging period due to the fallout from the **Middle East** conflict, which threatens to increase inflation and curb growth. The **ADB** forecasts that regional growth will moderate to 5.1% in both 2026 and 2027, with higher energy prices impacting production and consumer costs. While domestic demand remains solid in **South Asia** and developing **Southeast Asia**, the overall outlook is subject to significant uncertainty, especially if **Middle East** tensions persist longer than anticipated. Geopolitical tensions are also affecting capacity deployment within **Asia's** airline industry, with reduced capacity between **China** and **Japan** leading airlines to redeploy aircraft to **Southeast Asian** markets. The **International Monetary Fund (IMF)**, in its **April 2026 World Economic Outlook**, notes that defense spending is rising globally amid intensifying geopolitical tensions, with potential short-term economic boosts but also medium-term challenges like increased inflation.
