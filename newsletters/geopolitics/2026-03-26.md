## Middle East Escalation and Global Repercussions

Over the past 24-48 hours, the conflict in the **Middle East** has seen a qualitative escalation. Coordinated strikes by the **United States** and **Israel** have targeted strategic locations within **Iran**, including reports of the killing of **Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei** or **Ali Larijani**, a top security official. In response, **Iran** has reportedly launched missile and drone strikes across the region, targeting **Israeli** assets and **U.S.** military positions, as well as critical infrastructure in various **Gulf** countries such as **Saudi Arabia**, **Kuwait**, **Bahrain**, the **United Arab Emirates (UAE)**, and **Oman**.

A critical point of concern is the **Strait of Hormuz**, a vital maritime chokepoint. **Iran** has asserted its control over access to the strait, while **Iran**-backed **Houthi** rebels have signaled a resumption of attacks on shipping in the **Red Sea**. This has direct implications for global energy supply, with oil prices rising and concerns about sustained energy-driven inflation. The potential for disruption in the **Strait of Hormuz** would trigger a broad repricing across energy, inflation, and interest rates, posing a dilemma for central banks like the **Federal Reserve** in balancing inflation containment with economic stability. The global cybersecurity landscape is also being reshaped, with an increase in state-aligned cyberattacks targeting critical infrastructure and businesses worldwide.

## Diplomatic Engagements and Regional Adjustments

Amidst the escalating tensions, diplomatic channels are being explored. There have been reports of a proposed 15-point peace plan from the **U.S.** government to **Iran**, and speculation about a potential ceasefire, although **Iran** has reportedly rejected terms without certain assurances from the **U.S.**. The **G7 Foreign Ministers' Meeting** is currently taking place in **France**, where **U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio** is expected to provide clarity on **U.S.** and **Israeli** military operations against **Iran** and the existence of meaningful diplomatic channels to de-escalate the conflict. The **UN** human rights chief has warned against the escalating conflict spiraling into a broader crisis and urged states to uphold the rules-based international order.

In other regional developments, **Jordan's** Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs, **Ayman Safadi**, stated that relations with **Syria** have reached their highest levels across political, security, military, and economic fields. This includes a recent meeting in **Damascus** with **Syrian President Ahmad al-Sharaa** as part of a strategic dialogue. Meanwhile, the **Philippines**, facing energy security concerns due to the **Middle East** conflict, is exploring new fuel suppliers, including potentially **Russia**, and is open to restarting discussions with **China** on joint oil and gas exploration in the **South China Sea**. **President Ferdinand "Bongbong" Marcos Jr.** indicated a need to reset ties with **China**.

Beyond the **Middle East**, other significant events include continued hostilities in **Ukraine**, with **Russian** authorities reporting a fire at the **Baltic Sea** port of **Ust-Luga** following an overnight **Ukrainian** drone strike. Additionally, **Belarusian President Alexander Lukashenko** visited **Pyongyang**, **North Korea**, to meet with **Supreme Leader Kim Jong Un**, in an effort to improve relations between the two countries. These events underscore a broader global environment marked by heightened geopolitical risks and their far-reaching economic and security implications.
